Grantsville Citizens Protecting Property Rights — Community Update

The City's claim over private irrigation shares is an overreach impacting residents today — and potentially everyone tomorrow.

What’s happening

Grantsville City is asserting ownership of private irrigation shares and requiring “shares or fees in lieu” for a service it does not provide. About 1,700 households have already been notified, with many more likely affected if this continues.

Key points

  • City is claiming ownership of private irrigation shares historically held by residents.
  • Demanding “shares or fees in lieu.” Under Utah’s Impact Fees Act, a charge imposed as a condition of approval is an impact fee even if called something else. See Utah Code §11-36a-204 (Other names for impact fees); also §11-36a-202 (Prohibitions on impact fees).
  • City ordinance update: The newly adopted ordinance states the City may hold transferred water rights or shares “for future use on the property or within the City.” Read the ordinance: Grantsville Ord. 2024-11 (PDF).
    “…transfer to the City of water rights or shares to be held by the City for future use on the property or within the City.”
  • ~1,700 households notified; thousands more could be impacted.
  • Erodes property rights and property value.
  • Sets a precedent for further government overreach citywide.
  • City uses taxpayer resources; residents must self-fund their defense.
